What is BAC?

Alcohol can affect people differently based on variations in physiology and alcohol tolerance. One of the more direct ways to see how intoxicated a person is is by checking their BAC, or blood alcohol content. BAC is how much alcohol is in a person's blood stream. Below is an equation to compute an estimation of a person's BAC in percentage by volume.

Equation for estimated blood alcohol content (EBAC)

$EBAC = \left(\frac{0.086\times D\times 1.2 }{B\times W} - M \times T \right)$

0.806 and 1.2 are constants.

D is number of drinks.

B is body water constant - 0.58 for males, 0.49 for females.

W is weight in kilograms.

M is metabolism constant - 0.015 for males, 0.017 for females.

T is time in hours.

The Effects of Alcohol in Relation to Your BAC

When your BAC is:

0.02 - 0.059% ~ Relaxation, mild euphoria, minor impairment.

0.06 - 0.099% ~ Euphoria, slight impairment of balance, speech, reaction time. It is illegal to drive with a BAC of 0.08% in the state of California.

0.10 - 0.199% ~ Significant impairment of motor coordination, slurred speech. Nausea may appear.

0.20 - 0.299% ~ Severe motor impairment, emotional swings, vomiting, memory blackouts.

0.30 - 0.399% ~ Central nervous system depression, unresponsiveness, loss of understanding, trouble with breathing.

0.40 - 0.50% ~ Severe central nervous system depression, coma, low heart rates, possibility of death.

Higher than 0.50% ~ high possiblity of death.

Despite what myths (regarding coffee, cold showers, etc) may say, time is the only factor thing that will lower your BAC. Since drinking alcohol causes dehydration, it would be very wise to drink a plentiful amount of water, however drinking water does not sober you up faster.
